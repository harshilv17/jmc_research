The Essence

Not fashion. Tradition.

“Where every hand tells a story, and every piece comes from people who put their heart into it. This isn't just fashion. This is tradition.”

Brand voice: bold, founder-led, reverent of craft. Built on a Shiva philosophy — stillness, depth, the five elements — never literal iconography. The site is a slow, cinematic digital atelier, not a catalog.

Colour Palette

Drawn from the house itself

Every colour is pulled from something the brand already owns — the logo, the flagship buildings, the embroidery, the editorial backdrops. Nothing invented. Dark-mode-first, so gold and teal can glow.

Deep Teal
#163F3B
The brand's true colour — logo ground, the teal flagship buildings, the Shiva-philosophy showroom (AD-featured). The identity anchor: calm, regal, the water/depth element.
Antique Gold
#C9A24B
Zardozi thread and the wordmark itself. Carries craft, warmth and the "divine radiance" of the sunburst. The single accent — used sparingly so it stays precious.
Ivory / Buttercream
#F3EDE1
The raw canvas hand-painters work on; the "buttercream sherwani" of the Vogue wedding. Editorial breathing room — light relief inside a dark experience.
Oxblood
#5A1B1C
The bandhgalas and BOHO embellishment. Royal, theatrical depth — the colour of ceremony and drama. Used for moments, not surfaces.
Muted Olive
#4A4E3A
The painterly portrait backdrops (Zayn / ELLE). A quiet editorial neutral that flatters skin and gold — natural section ground.
Terracotta Rust
#A65A2E
The warm "Occasion Edit" backdrop on their own site. Indian-summer warmth — an accent for occasion/festive chapters.
Earth Brown
#5A4632
The earthy silk kurtas and androgynous styling. Grounds the palette in handloom and skin — never sterile.
Charcoal Base
#141312
The shadow in every reel and the draped-silk hero. The cinematic ground the whole site sits on, so video and gold lead the eye.
Charcoal 50% Teal 30% Ivory 10% Gold 6% Oxblood 4%

Suggested working ratio — a dark, teal-grounded canvas; ivory for editorial passages; gold + oxblood strictly as accents.
